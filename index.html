In Canada, they will restrict the use of English



In the Canadian province of Quebec, public services will be provided exclusively in French. This was stated by the Prime Minister of the province Francois Legolt, Ukrinform reports.

"If your parents went to an English-language school, then you have certain rights in Quebec, and we will respect them. If you are a new immigrant, then we must speak French with you," Legolt said.

He added that to ensure the implementation of this decision, the government of Quebec does not need to make legislative changes, it is enough to apply the already adopted provisions of Law No. 101, which establishes rules for the use of languages in the province.

This decision was severely criticized by representatives of the opposition parties.

Note that at the federal level, Canada is a bilingual country, so Canadians can receive all services and information from the central government in English or French.

"At the same time, Canadian provinces and territories have the right to independently choose their official language, and in the case of Quebec, it is only French," the report says.

The province has a large English-speaking minority but places great importance on language proficiency in selecting new immigrants.
